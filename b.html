Miao’s construction with good corners

The presence of corners and singular metrics greatly complicates the construction of the smoothing procedure. Let \(\Sigma\) be the round boundary component of the domain \(\Omega\). By using orthogonal foliation of \(\Sigma\) (to \(\pi\)) to the left and to the right, a coordinate \(t\) can be induced from this foliation. Since every leaf of the foliation is not neccessailiy equidistant, the metric has the following form, \[g = u^2 (x, t) d t^2 + 2 u_i (x, t) d t d x^i + g_{i j} (x, t) d x^i d x^j . \label{interior}\] Note that with the case of equidistant foliation, \(u \equiv 1\) and \(u_i\) vanishes.

 

In this setting, \(u_i\) vanishes identically along \(\pi\) since the foliation is orthogonal.

Note that we can choose \[u (x, 0) = 1, u_i (x, 0) = 0,\] which is just that \(X\) restricted to \(\Sigma\) is \(\partial_t\).

We also have to pick a coordinate near \(\partial \Sigma\). Both metric \(g_+\) and \(g_-\) induces the same metric on \(\Sigma\), hence on \(\partial \Sigma\) as well. We use the Fermi coordinate near \(\partial \Sigma\). That is when \(t = 0\), \[g_{i j} (x, 0) d x^i d x^j = d r^2 + h_{A B} (y, r) .\] The Fermi coordinate near \(\partial \Sigma\) in \(\Sigma\) can be extended along the foliation, this gives a metric near a neighbouhood in \(\Omega\) of \(\partial \Sigma\) and the neighborhood admit a coordinate patch \(\{t, r, y^A \}\). In general, the metric \(g = g_+ \cup g_-\) takes the following form
