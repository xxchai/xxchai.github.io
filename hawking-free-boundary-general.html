GENERALIZED HAWKING MASS WITH BOUNDARY

Wed, Apr 14, 2021 Xiaoxiang Chai

Generalized Hawking mass with boundary is the following

\(\displaystyle m_H (S) = \sqrt{|S|} (8 \pi \chi (S) - \int_S (H^2 + \tfrac{2}{3} \Lambda_1) - \int_{\partial S} \tfrac{4}{3} \Lambda_2) .\) (1)

Suppose that \(S\) sits inside a 3-manifold \(M\) with boundary, \(\partial S\) is nonempty, \(R_M \geqslant \Lambda_1\) and \(H_{\partial M} \geqslant \Lambda_2\). We show a Christodoulou-Yau estimate.

Let \(S\) be a volume preserving stable surface with free boundary. From second variation, we have that

\(\displaystyle \int_S | \nabla f|^2 - (\operatorname{Ric}(\nu) + |A|^2) f^2 - \int_{\partial S} B (\nu, \nu) f^2 \geqslant 0. \) (2)

Using Schoen-Yau trick, we have that

\(\displaystyle \operatorname{Ric} (\nu) + |A|^2 = \tfrac{1}{2} R_M + \tfrac{1}{2} |A^o |^2 + \tfrac{3}{4} H^2 - K.\) (3)

We have also along \(\partial S\) using the free boundary condition that

\(\displaystyle B (\nu, \nu) = H_{\partial M} - H_{\partial S} = H_{\partial M} - \kappa .\) (4)

We have a triple \(\psi_i\) with \(i = 1, 2, 3\) such that \(\int_S \psi_i = 0\), \(\sum_i \psi_i^2 = 1\), \(\int_S | \nabla \psi_i |^2 = 8 \pi \deg (\psi)\), we assume that \(S\) is a disc, so

\(\displaystyle \deg (\psi) \leqslant \tfrac{[g + 3]}{2} = 1.\) (5)

See [Ros and Vergasta, 1995]

With the lower bounds on \(R_M\) and \(H_{\partial M}\), we have

\(\displaystyle 8 \pi \geqslant \int_S \tfrac{1}{2} \Lambda_1 + \tfrac{3}{4} H^2 + \int_{\partial S} \Lambda_2 + (\int_S K + \int_{\partial S} \kappa) .\) (6)

Using Gauss-Bonnet theorem, we have that

\(\displaystyle 8 \pi \geqslant 2 \pi + \tfrac{3}{4} \int_S (H^2 + \tfrac{2}{3} \Lambda_1) + \int_{\partial S} \Lambda_2 .\) (7)

So we have that

\(\displaystyle 8 \pi - \int_S (H^2 + \tfrac{2}{3} \Lambda_1) - \int_{\partial S} \tfrac{4}{3} \Lambda_2 \geqslant 0.\) (8)

So we see that the Hawking mass

\(\displaystyle | \Sigma |^{\tfrac{1}{2}} \left( 1 - \tfrac{1}{8 \pi} \int_S (H^2 + \tfrac{2}{3} \Lambda_1) - \tfrac{1}{8 \pi} \int_{\partial S} \tfrac{4}{3} \Lambda_2 \right) \geqslant 0.\) (9)

The examples of ambient \(M\) are Euclidean balls, hyperbolic balls, horoballs, constant mean curvature surfaces and even \(B^2 \times \mathbb{S}^1\).

There are several questions related to the Hawking mass, we name a few.

Question For a disc \(S\) whose boundary lies in a timelike hypersurface, the spacetime Hawking energy with free boundary seems to be given by the following

\(\displaystyle |S|^{\tfrac{1}{2}} (1 - \tfrac{1}{8 \pi} \int_S | \vec{H} |^2)\) (10)

where \(\vec{H}\) is the mean curvature vector in spacetime. How to adapt the definition to the boundary case?
