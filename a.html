2 ADM mass and Schoen-Yau Positive Mass Theorem

2.1 Asymptotically flat manifold \( (M^3,g) \)

  • \(M\sim K\) diffeomorphic to \(\mathbb{R}^{3}\sim B\)
  • metric \[|g-\delta|+|\partial g|+|\partial\partial g|=O(r^{-1})\]
  • Abbreviation: AF manifolds

2.2 ADM mass

  • Arnowitt, Deser, Misner \[ m(g) = \int_{S_{\infty}} (g_{ij,j}-g_{jj,i})\nu^i \]
  • (Bartnik '86) geometric invariance: independent of asymptotic coordinates

2.3 Schoen-Yau Positive mass theorem

Using Minimal Surface techniques:

  • \((M^3,g)\) AF, \(R\geq0\)

Then

  • \(m(g)\geq 0\)
  • Equality iff \(M\) is isometric \(\mathbb{R}^3\)

2.4 quasi-local mass

A localized version of ADM mass for a \(2\)-surface \(\Sigma\)

  • \(H_0\): mean curvature of \(\Sigma\) isometric embedded in \(\mathbb{R}^3\)
  • \(H\): mean curvature of \(\Sigma\) in \(M\)

2.5 quasi-local mass

  1. Brown-York '92, '93

    \[ BY(\Sigma,g) = \int_{\Sigma} (H_0-H) \]

  2. Hawking

    \[ H(\Sigma,g) = \sqrt{\frac{|\Sigma|}{16\pi}}\left(1-\frac{1}{16\pi}\int_{\Sigma} H^2\right) \]

2.6 Other quasi-local mass

Wang-Yau, Liu-Yau, Bartnik

2.7 Positive mass theorem for Brown-York mass

  1. Shi-Tam 2003
    • \((\Sigma^2,g)\) bound a bounded region \(\Omega^3\)
    • \(\Sigma\), positive Gauss curvature and mean curvature
    • \(\Omega^3\) is of nonnegative scalar curvature

    Then

    • \(BY(\Sigma,g)\geq0\)
    • equality iff \(\Omega\) is an Euclidean domain.

    • Gauss equation: Let \( X \) be the position vector in \( \mathbb{R}^3 \)

    \[ G(X,\nu) \sim r G(\nu,\nu)=r(H^2-|A|^2-S)/2 \]

    • Using principal curvatures:

4 Shi-Tam result

4.1 Shi-Tam 2002

  • quasi-spherical metrics (Bartnik 1993)

    \(ds^2_0 =d\rho^{2} +g_{\rho} \) on \( N \times [a,\infty) \); new metric of the form \( ds^2 = u^2 d\rho^2 + g_{\rho} \) is called quasi-spherical metric

4.2 Shi-Tam 2002

  • foliation of zero scalar curvature: \[ 2H_0 \frac{\partial u}{\partial r} = 2u^2 \Delta_r u+(u-u^3) R^r.\label{foliation}\]
  • the equation is parabolic if \( H_0>0 \); initial data \( u(x,0) = u_0 \)

where \( u_0 \) is a smooth function on \( \Sigma_0 \).

4.3 Shi-Tam 2002 solution

  • Unique solution for initial value problem
  • \( ds^2 \) is AF with zero scalar curvature outside \( \Sigma_0 \).
  • asymptotics of \( u \): \[ u(z) = 1+m_0\rho^{2-n} + v, \]

where \( m_0 \) is a constant, \( v \) with \( |v|+\rho|\nabla_0 v|=O(\rho^{1-n}) \).

4.4 Shi-Tam 2002 solution

  • \[ m_{ADM} \sim m_0 \sim \int_{S_{\infty}} H_0(1-u^{-1}) = \int_{S_{\infty}} (H_0-H). \]
  • Let \[ M(r) = \int_{\Sigma_r} H_0(1-u^{-1}) d\sigma_r, \] then \( M(r) \) is nondecreasing.

4.5 extensions

  • Extension of Brown-York mass: Liu-Yau mass \( \int_{\Sigma} (H_0-\sqrt{H^2-(tr_{\Sigma}p)^2}); \) Wang-Yau mass (2008)
  • Wang-Yau 2006, A generalization of Liu-Yau quasi-local mass. Equation of foliation with prescribed scalar curvature \( R=-6 \): \[ \sinh(2\rho) \frac{\partial u}{\partial \rho} = u^2 \Delta_{\mathbb{S}^2}u+(u-u^3)(1+3 \sinh^2 \rho). \]
  • Lu-Miao (jdg, 2017) to static case, application to a quasi-local version of Penrose inequality: \( \int V(H_0-H) \) has a lower bound by \( c(n) \sqrt{|\Sigma_0|}. \)

5 Wang-Yau mass

5.1 Wang-Yau mass 2009

  • Let \( e_3 \) be a spacelike unit normal along \( \Sigma \); connection form determined by

\( e_3 \) is \[ \alpha_{e_3}(X) = \langle \nabla_X e_3,e_4 \rangle, \] where \( e_4 \) is the future directed timelike unit normal orthogonal to \( e_3 \).

  • generalized mean curvature \[ h(\Sigma,i,\tau,e_3)=-\sqrt{1+|\nabla \tau|^2} \langle H,e_3 \rangle -\alpha_{e_3}(\nabla \tau) .\]

5.2 Wang-Yau mass 2009

Let \( e_3,e_4 \) vary through all choices \[ e_3 = \cosh \phi \hat{e}_3 - \sinh \phi \hat{e}_4, e_4 = -\sinh \phi \hat{e}_3 + \cosh \phi \hat{e}_4. \] The integral \( \int_{\Sigma} h(\Sigma,i,\tau,e_3) \) is then a functional of \( \phi \). The minimum is \[ W(\Sigma,i,\tau)=\int_{\Sigma} \sqrt{(\Delta\tau)^2 + |H|^2(1+|\nabla\tau|^2)} - \nabla\tau\cdot\nabla\phi - \alpha_{\hat{e}_3}(\nabla\tau). \] With the choice of \( \hat{e}_3 = -H |H|^{-1} \), \[ W(\Sigma,i,\tau) =-\int_{\Sigma} \sqrt{1+|\nabla\tau|^2}\langle H, \bar{e}_3 \rangle + \alpha_{\bar{e}_3}(\nabla\tau).\]

5.3 Wang-Yau mass 2009

Wang-Yau mass

\( WY \) infimum of \[ W(\Sigma,i_0,\tau) - W(\Sigma,i,\tau) ,\] where \( i_0 \) is the unique isometric embedding into \( \mathbf{R}^{3,1} \).
